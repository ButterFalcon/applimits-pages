Getting Started

What is Applimits?

Applimits is an iOS app designed to help you manage your smartphone usage and improve focus. You can set time limits for specific apps or categories, block apps during bedtime, and use the Pomodoro timer to stay concentrated on your work.

Key Features:

  • Schedule: Limit daily usage time by day of week (0-240 minutes)
  • Downtime: Block apps during specific time periods
  • Focus Timer: Pomodoro-style timer to boost concentration
  • Hard Limitation: A mode that prevents immediate removal of restrictions
How do I get started with Applimits?

Step 1: Open the app and follow the on-screen instructions to grant Screen Time permissions. This permission is required.

Step 2: Tap the "+" button on the home screen to create a new schedule.

Step 3: Select the apps or categories you want to limit (free version: up to 2, Pro: unlimited).

Step 4: Set the days and time limits. You can also use presets (weekdays/weekends/everyday).

Step 5: Tap "Save" to complete the setup.

What's the difference between Schedule and Downtime?

Schedule: Sets a daily usage time limit for specific apps or categories. Example: "Limit social media to 30 minutes per day on weekdays."

Downtime: Completely blocks selected apps or categories during specific time periods. Example: "Block social media from 10:00 PM to 7:00 AM." Perfect for managing bedtime.

Key Differences:

  • Schedule: Limits total daily usage time
  • Downtime: Completely blocks during specific hours
What's the difference between the free and Pro plans?

  • Schedules: Up to 6

Schedule Features

How do I set time limits for schedules?

Schedules allow you to set time limits from 0 to 240 minutes (0-4 hours) per day.

Configuration:

  • Set different time limits for each day of the week
  • Use presets (weekdays/weekends/everyday) for bulk setup
  • Setting 0 minutes completely blocks apps for that day

Example: "30 minutes Mon-Fri, 60 minutes Sat-Sun"

What does "Exclude Today" mean?

The "Exclude Today" feature allows you to delay the start of a schedule until tomorrow.

Use cases:

  • You need to use an app today for special reasons but want restrictions from tomorrow
  • You want to prepare schedules in advance

Note: When "Exclude Today" is enabled, restrictions won't apply today. To enable the schedule for today, use "Re-register All Schedules" in settings.

What happens when I reach my time limit?

When you reach your time limit, the selected apps or categories will be automatically blocked.

During restriction:

  • Attempting to open a restricted app will show the iOS Screen Time screen
  • If "Hard Limitation" is not enabled, you can tap "Ignore Limit" to temporarily bypass
  • Limits reset at 0:00 (midnight) the next day
Which apps are affected when I restrict a category?

When you select a category, all apps within that category are automatically restricted.

Available category examples:

  • Social Networking (Twitter, Instagram, Facebook, etc.)
  • Entertainment (YouTube, Netflix, TikTok, etc.)
  • Games (all game apps)
  • And many more categories

When you install a new app in a restricted category, it's automatically included in the restriction.

Downtime Features

When is Downtime most effective?

Downtime completely blocks apps during specific time periods. It's most effective for:

Bedtime: Block social media from 10:00 PM to 7:00 AM to improve sleep quality

Study time: Block games and video apps from 7:00 PM to 9:00 PM

Late night prevention: Block entertainment apps from 0:00 AM to 6:00 AM to prevent staying up late

Downtime is unlimited even in the free version.

Can Downtime span overnight?

Yes, it can. Downtime can be set to span across midnight.

Example: Setting "10:00 PM - 7:00 AM" will block apps from 10 PM until 7 AM the next morning.

Presets:

  • Bedtime (10:00 PM - 7:00 AM)
  • Study Time (7:00 PM - 9:00 PM)
  • Late Night (0:00 AM - 6:00 AM)

You can also set different downtime periods for different days of the week.

Can I use Schedule and Downtime together?

Yes, Schedule and Downtime can be used together for different purposes.

Combined example:

  • Schedule: "Limit social media to 30 minutes per day" to control daytime usage
  • Downtime: "Block social media from 10:00 PM to 7:00 AM" to ensure bedtime

This combination helps prevent both daytime overuse and late-night browsing.

Focus Timer Features

What is the Focus Timer?

Focus Timer is a feature that uses the Pomodoro technique to enhance concentration (Pro plan exclusive).

What is the Pomodoro technique? A time management method that alternates 25-minute focus sessions with 5-minute breaks to maintain high concentration.

How to use:

  1. Open the Focus Timer tab
  2. Select apps to restrict
  3. Set timer duration (15 min, 30 min, 1 hour, or custom)
  4. Set break time
  5. Use loop function to repeat the timer
What is the minimum timer duration?

The minimum Focus Timer duration is 15 minutes.

Preset durations:

  • 15 minutes
  • 30 minutes
  • 1 hour
  • Custom (15 minutes or more)

This is by design to ensure meaningful focus time. For restrictions shorter than 15 minutes, please use Schedule or Downtime features.

Will I receive a notification when the timer ends?

Yes, you'll receive a notification when the timer ends (if notification permission is granted).

Notification content:

  • Timer completion notification
  • Break time start notification

Note: Notification permission is optional, but without it you won't receive timer completion notifications. We recommend granting notification permission at first launch or in settings.

Hard Limitation

What is Hard Limitation?

Hard Limitation is a powerful restriction feature that, once enabled, cannot be disabled until 0:00 (midnight) the next day (Pro plan exclusive).

Features:

  • Once enabled, settings cannot be changed or deleted until 0:00 the next day
  • Available for Schedules, Focus Timer, and Downtime
  • Strengthens self-control and creates an environment resistant to temptation

Important: Please review your settings carefully before enabling Hard Limitation. Once activated, it cannot be undone even in emergencies.

Can Hard Limitation really not be disabled?

Yes, once Hard Limitation is enabled, it cannot be disabled until 0:00 (midnight) the next day. This is intentional.

When it's unlocked:

  • It automatically unlocks at 0:00 (midnight) the next day
  • Until then, it cannot be disabled even by deleting the app or restarting the device

Recommendation: If you're using this feature for the first time, try starting with short-duration restrictions.

Does Hard Limitation work differently for Schedule vs Downtime?

No, the basic behavior is the same. For all features, once Hard Limitation is enabled, it cannot be disabled until 0:00 the next day.

For Schedule: Cannot change or delete time limits or day settings.

For Downtime: Cannot change or delete blocked hours or target apps.

For Focus Timer: Cannot stop the timer or change target apps.

In all cases, no changes can be made until 0:00 the next day.

Troubleshooting

My restrictions aren't working

If your restrictions aren't working, please check the following in order:

1. Verify Screen Time permissions

  • Go to iPhone "Settings" → "Screen Time" → "Applimits" and verify permissions are enabled
  • If disabled, grant permission again from the Applimits app

2. Check schedule settings

  • Are the target days enabled?
  • Is the time limit set to something other than 0 minutes?
  • Is "Exclude Today" enabled?

3. Restart your device

  • After iOS updates, restart your iPhone

4. Re-register schedules

  • Tap "Re-register All Schedules" in the settings screen
Restrictions stopped working after an iOS update

If restrictions stop working after an iOS update, try the following:

1. Restart your device (most effective)

  • Completely restart your iPhone
  • This solves the problem in most cases

2. Re-register schedules

  • Open Applimits Settings screen
  • Tap "Re-register All Schedules"
  • All schedules and Downtime settings will be re-applied

3. Re-verify Screen Time permissions

  • Go to iPhone "Settings" → "Screen Time" → "Applimits" and verify permissions
What does "Re-register All Schedules" do?

"Re-register All Schedules" re-applies all schedule and Downtime settings to iOS.

When to use:

  • When restrictions aren't working properly
  • After an iOS update
  • When you want to disable "Exclude Today" and enable restrictions for today

How to do it:

  1. Open the Settings screen
  2. Tap "Re-register All Schedules"
  3. Tap "OK" in the confirmation dialog

Note: This operation re-applies all schedule and Downtime settings. Your app settings are preserved.

I can't edit or delete a schedule

Main reasons you can't edit or delete a schedule:

Hard Limitation is enabled

  • When Hard Limitation is enabled, you cannot make changes until 0:00 the next day
  • At 0:00 the next day, you'll automatically be able to edit or delete
  • This is an intentional feature designed to strengthen self-control

Focus Timer is running

  • You need to wait until the timer ends
  • If Hard Limitation is enabled, you can't stop the timer mid-session
Can I prevent Applimits from being deleted?

You can use iOS Screen Time features to prevent deletion of Applimits itself:

  1. Open the "Settings" app on your iPhone
  2. Tap "Screen Time"
  3. Tap "Content & Privacy Restrictions"
  4. Tap "iTunes & App Store Purchases"
  5. Set "Deleting Apps" to "Don't Allow"

Recommendation: Setting a Screen Time passcode provides even stronger protection. Having family or trusted friends manage the passcode is even more effective.

What happens to my schedules if I cancel Pro?

When you cancel Pro and return to the free version:

Schedules:

  • Existing schedules are preserved, but the 2nd and later schedules will be disabled
  • Only the 1st schedule remains active

App selection:

  • If you had 3 or more apps selected, only the first 2 will remain active

Downtime:

  • All Downtime settings remain active (unlimited even in free version)

Focus Timer & Hard Limitation:

  • These Pro features become unavailable

Privacy & Security

What permissions does Applimits need?

Applimits requires the following permissions:

Screen Time permission: Required

  • Necessary to restrict app usage time
  • Required for all Applimits features to function
  • The app will not work without this permission

Notification permission: Optional

  • Necessary to receive Focus Timer completion notifications
  • The app will work without this permission, but you won't receive timer notifications
  • Can be granted later from the settings screen
How does Applimits handle my data?

Applimits prioritizes your privacy:

  • All data is stored on your device
  • Schedule settings and app usage information are not sent externally
  • Subscription information is managed by Apple (Applimits cannot access it)
  • We do not collect personally identifiable information
  • We do not collect analytics data
