Troubleshooting

My restrictions are not working properly

⚠ For iOS 26 Users

A bug has been reported in iOS 26 where App Time Limits (Screen Time App Limits) do not work correctly. For details, please refer to the Apple Developer Forum.

If this issue occurs frequently, please consider using "Scheduled Restriction" or "Restrict Now" until the bug is fixed.

If restrictions are not working, please check the following:

1. (iPhone/iPad users) Disable Share Across Devices

  • Apple has reported that sharing Screen Time across devices may cause instability
  • We recommend disabling "Share Across Devices" in iPhone Settings → Screen Time
  • We recommend disabling "Share Across Devices" in Mac Settings → Screen Time
  • Even if you disable it on iPhone, if it remains enabled on Mac, "Share Across Devices" may be re-enabled. We recommend disabling it on all devices

2. (iPhone/iPad users) Check Screen Time permissions

  • Go to iPhone Settings → Screen Time → Applimits and check that permissions are enabled
  • If permissions are disabled, please grant them again from the Applimits app

3. (iPhone/iPad users) For MDM users

  • On devices managed by MDM such as Google Device Policy or Intune, the MDM sync timing may cause issues with in-app time limit settings
  • If issues occur, please run "Re-register Schedules" from the settings screen

4. Re-register Schedules

  • If you change your device's Screen Time or other settings, the app's settings may be removed.
  • Please tap "Re-register Schedules" in the settings screen to reapply your settings

5. Check schedule settings

  • Are the target days of the week enabled?
  • Is the time limit set to something other than 0 minutes?
  • Is "Disable for Today" turned on?
Restrictions stopped working after an OS update

If restrictions stop working after an OS update, please try the following:

1. Restart your device

  • Completely restart your device

2. Re-register Schedules

  • Open the Applimits settings screen
  • Tap "Re-register Schedules"
  • All schedules and downtime settings will be reapplied

3. Re-check Screen Time permissions

  • Go to iPhone Settings → Screen Time → Applimits and verify permissions
What is "Re-register Schedules"?

"Re-register Schedules" is a feature that reapplies all schedule and downtime settings.

When to use:

  • When restrictions are not working properly
  • After an OS update

How to run:

  1. Open the settings screen
  2. Tap "Re-register Schedules"
  3. Tap "OK" in the confirmation dialog

Note: This operation reapplies all schedule and downtime settings. Your in-app settings are preserved.

(iPhone/iPad) Can I prevent the Applimits app from being deleted?

You can use the iPhone/iPad Screen Time feature to prevent Applimits from being deleted:

  1. Open the "Settings" app on your iPhone
  2. Tap "Screen Time"
  3. Tap "Content & Privacy Restrictions"
  4. Tap "iTunes & App Store Purchases"
  5. Set "Deleting Apps" to "Don't Allow"

Recommended: Setting a Screen Time passcode provides stronger protection. Having a family member or trusted person manage the passcode makes it even more effective.

Getting Started

What is Applimits?

Applimits is an app for managing your smartphone usage time and helping you stay focused. You can set time limits for specific apps, block app usage during bedtime, and concentrate on your work with a Pomodoro timer.

Main features:

  • Usage Time Limit: Set daily usage time by day of the week
  • Restrict Now: Immediately restrict apps with a timer
  • Scheduled Restriction: Set time periods when apps cannot be used
  • Hard Restriction: A powerful mode that prevents immediate removal of restrictions
How do I get started?

Step 1: Open the app and follow the on-screen instructions to grant Screen Time permissions. This permission is required.

Step 2: Tap the "+" button on the home screen to create a new setting.

Step 3: Select the apps or categories you want to restrict.

Step 4: Set the days of the week and time limits. You can also use presets (weekdays/weekends/everyday).

Step 5: Tap "Save" and you're done.

What is the difference between Schedule and Downtime?

Usage Time Limit: Sets a maximum daily usage time for specific apps or categories. Example: "Limit social media to 30 minutes per day on weekdays."

Scheduled Restriction: Completely blocks selected apps during specific time periods. Example: "Block social media from 10:00 PM to 7:00 AM." Ideal for managing bedtime.

Key differences:

  • Schedule: Limits total daily usage time
  • Downtime: Completely blocks during specific time periods
What is the difference between the free version and the Pro plan?

Free version:

  • Schedules: Up to 1
  • Hard Restriction: Not available

Pro plan:

  • Schedules: Up to 6
  • Hard Restriction: Available

Usage Time Limit

How do I set usage time limits?

You can set daily app usage time limits.

How to set up:

  • If multiple apps are selected, restrictions are applied when the combined usage exceeds the set time
  • You can set different time limits for each day of the week
  • Use presets (weekdays/weekends/everyday) for easy bulk settings
  • Setting 0 minutes completely blocks apps for that day

Example: You can set "30 minutes Mon-Fri, 60 minutes Sat-Sun."

What is "Disable for Today"?

The "Disable for Today" feature lets you remove today's restriction and set it to start from the next day.

Use case:

  • You need to use an app today for special reasons but want to restrict it from tomorrow
What happens when I reach the time limit?

When you reach the set time limit, the selected apps or categories are automatically blocked.

Behavior during restriction:

  • When you try to open a restricted app, the iPhone/iPad Screen Time screen is displayed
  • If "Hard Restriction" is not enabled, you can temporarily remove it with "Disable for Today"
  • The time limit resets at 0:00 the next day and becomes active again
(iPhone/iPad) Which apps are affected when restricting by category?

When you select a category, all apps in that category are automatically restricted.

Available category examples:

  • Social Networking (Twitter, Instagram, Facebook, etc.)
  • Entertainment (YouTube, Netflix, TikTok, etc.)
  • Games (all game apps)
  • And many more categories

If you install a new app in a category, it is automatically included in the restriction.

Scheduled Restriction

When is Scheduled Restriction most effective?

This feature completely blocks apps during specific time periods. It is effective in situations such as:

Bedtime: Block social media from 10:00 PM to 7:00 AM to improve sleep quality

Study time: Block games and video apps from 7:00 PM to 9:00 PM

Late night prevention: Block entertainment apps from 0:00 AM to 6:00 AM to prevent staying up late

Can Scheduled Restriction span across midnight?

Yes, it can. You can set it to span across midnight.

Example: Setting "10:00 PM – 7:00 AM" will block apps from 10 PM until 7 AM the next morning.

Presets:

  • Bedtime (10:00 PM – 7:00 AM)
  • Study time (7:00 PM – 9:00 PM)
  • Late night (0:00 AM – 6:00 AM)

You can also set different schedules for each day of the week.

Restrict Now

What is Restrict Now?

It is a feature that helps you improve focus using a timer and the Pomodoro technique.

What is the Pomodoro technique: A time management method that maintains high concentration by alternating 25-minute focused work sessions with 5-minute breaks.

How to use:

  1. Select the apps you want to restrict
  2. Set the timer duration (15 minutes, 30 minutes, 1 hour, or custom)
  3. Enable Pomodoro
  4. Set the break time
Will I receive a notification when the timer ends?

Yes, you will receive a notification when the timer ends (if notification permission is granted).

Notification content:

  • Timer completion notification
  • Start of the set break time

Note: Notification permission is optional, but without it, you will not receive timer completion notifications. We recommend granting notification permission at first launch or from the settings screen.

Hard Restriction

What is Hard Restriction?

Hard Restriction is a powerful restriction feature that, once enabled, cannot be disabled until 0:00 the next day (Pro plan only).

Features:

  • Once enabled, settings cannot be changed or deleted until 0:00 the next day
  • Available for schedules, focus timer, and downtime
  • Strengthens self-control and creates an environment resistant to temptation

Note: Please carefully review your settings before enabling Hard Restriction. Once enabled, it cannot be undone. (If you absolutely need to disable it, please delete the app.)

Can Hard Restriction really not be disabled?

Yes, once Hard Restriction is enabled, it cannot be disabled until 0:00 (midnight) the next day. This is an intentional design.

When it is released:

  • It is automatically released at 0:00 (midnight) the next day
  • Until then, it cannot be disabled even by restarting the device
  • You can disable it by deleting the app, so in case of emergency please delete the app
